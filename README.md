## Technical Specifications That Actually Matter

Marketing materials love throwing around buzzwords without explaining what they mean in practical terms. Let's dig into the technical specifications that genuinely impact your experience with DigiRDP's infrastructure.

**SSD vs. NVMe Storage:**

DigiRDP emphasizes SSD storage across their product line, but not all SSDs are created equal. Traditional SATA SSDs connect through the same interface as hard drives, limiting maximum throughput to around 600 MB/s. NVMe (Non-Volatile Memory Express) SSDs connect directly to the PCIe bus, delivering sequential read speeds exceeding 3,500 MB/s and dramatically lower latency for small random operations.

Their premium plans, particularly the India Ryzen VPS and higher-tier dedicated servers, utilize NVMe storage. This matters significantly for databases performing lots of random reads and writes, virtual machine hosts running multiple operating systems, or development environments compiling large codebases. The performance difference between SATA SSD and NVMe becomes obvious when you're waiting for a database query to complete or a Docker image to build.
